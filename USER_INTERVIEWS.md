# User Interviews

Three interviews conducted with working developers at MNC and startups,
May 2026. Each interview was 10-15 minutes via call & WhatsApp's chat.

---

## Interview 1 — Manuj Takkar

**Date:** May 2026  
**Name:** Manuj Takkar  
**Role:** SDE-1  
**Company:** SunTech Technology  
**Company stage:** Small team, active AI tool users  

**Summary:**  
Manuj's team pays for Claude and GitHub Copilot Pro. He personally 
spends around ₹1600/month on AI tools. His team decides on plans 
based on team needs rather than any formal audit process.

**Direct quotes:**
- "We just go with whatever the team needs, no one really tracks it"
- "I never felt like we were overpaying, but I also never checked"
- "₹1600 a month feels okay but I don't know if there's something cheaper"

**Most surprising thing:**  
Manuj said he had never felt overpaying — but when I asked if he had 
ever compared prices or checked alternatives, he said no. This revealed 
that the problem is not that people know they are overpaying and ignore it 
— they simply never think to check. The pain is invisible.

## Interview 2 — Divyansh

**Date:** May 2026  
**Name:** Divyansh  
**Role:** Frontend Developer  
**Company:** Catalys  
**Company stage:** Small startup  

**Summary:**  
Divyansh's team uses Copilot and Claude. He personally doesn't pay 
for any AI tools — his company covers it. This means he has no 
visibility into what the company actually spends or whether it's optimal.

**Direct quotes:**
- "I just use what the company gives me, I don't see the bill"
- "Copilot and Claude, that's what everyone uses at our place"
- "I wouldn't know if we were overpaying, that's someone else's job"

**Most surprising thing:**  
Divyansh had zero visibility into AI tool costs even though he uses 
them daily. This was surprising — the person using the tools has no 
idea what they cost. This means the real user of SpendLens is the 
engineering manager or CTO, not the individual developer. The 
developer is the beneficiary, not the buyer.

## Interview 3 — Ritesh

**Date:** May 2026  
**Name:** Ritesh  
**Role:** SDE-1  
**Company:** Beema Plan  
**Company stage:** 90-110 person company  

**Summary:**  
Ritesh works at a larger company of 90-110 people using Claude 
Enterprise. The plan decision was straightforward — team size 
determined the plan. No personal AI spend since everything is 
company-provided.

**Direct quotes:**
- "We are 90-110 people so enterprise just makes sense"
- "We pay well on AI, it's worth it for the productivity"
- "At our scale someone in finance handles this, not me"

**Most surprising thing:**  
Ritesh said "we pay well on AI" with confidence — but when I asked 
who specifically tracks whether they're on the right plan or getting 
value, he was not sure. At 60-70 people, AI tool decisions get 
delegated to finance, who don't understand the tools, while 
developers who understand the tools don't see the costs. There's 
a gap in the middle that nobody owns.

---

## What I fell 
1. **The pain is invisible** — nobody felt they were overpaying, 
   but nobody had ever checked either
2. **Individual developers don't see the bill** — the buyer and 
   user are different people
3. **The real buyer is the CTO/EM** — not the developer using the tool
